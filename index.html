About us

About Us

Bethesda Youth Welfare Center is one of the pioneer organizations in Nagaland committed for the upliftment of the people afflicted and inflicted by the problem of Substance Abuse. It was founded and established in September, 1987 as a non Non-Profit Charitable Organization. Bethesda YWC is registered with Societies Registration Act XXI of 1860. 80 G, 12A Income Tax Act-1961 and FCRA under the Govt. of India Acts. Over the years, it has incorporated several programs in its area of work such as HIV/AIDS, Women and Child Health Care, Rural Development and Health, Research, Protection of Environment, Anti-Human Trafficking, promotion and protection of cultural heritage, flora and fauna as per the need of time. Conducting Counseling, De-Addiction and Rehabilitation, Targeted intervention for IDUs, Substitution Therapy and conducting of trainings are other activities within Bethesda YWC.
Today, Bethesda YWC family consists of 52 major staffs, 34 Peer Educators, 4 part time Medical Officers. The organization has network and partnering with various local, national and international organization and is/was supported by the Government of India Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, UNODC, NSACS/NACO, AVAHAN(BGMF)EHA-GFATM-Rd-9(Project HIFAZAT),NEIHRN, Northeast Dialogue Forum, N-Naga DAO, IHRN, IPACHA, and Grace Network International, All Angel’s Church(Love People’s Mission),Bread for the World(Bftw),and World Diabetes Foundation(WDF),FINGO-DAP,IMPULSE NGO Network, Action against Trafficking and Sexual Exploitation of Children(ATSEC),YRG Care etc.
Bethesda YWC has drawn up the plans for a befitting decade of its rich history, to remember and in adoration of its founders and builders, and to set in the notion schemes that will take Bethesda YWC higher in its pursuit of excellence in the field of sustainable holistic service delivery system.

Vision

Rebuilding the decadent society for a better tomorrow.

Mission

Bethesda Youth Welfare Centre strives to bring hope into the lives of our brothers and sisters, affected and infected by Drugs and HIV/AIDS related problems, the poor and the needy through education, rehabilitation, and health facilities, and to promote socio-economic justice to the marginalized community and create a conducive environment for promoting holistic development.

Aims and Objectives

  • To bring deliverance and promote spiritual, physical, mental, educational development and upliftment of the young people who are dying in the stroms of Drug forces.
  • To establish any home, institutions for the welfare, maintenance, treatment, counseling, rehabilitation and development for the wayward young boys and girls.
  • To aid or establish any institution or adopt means for imparting, promotion of any art, science or any other field of education, propagate for removal of social evils, customs or render or promote self-help to overcome any of these evils particularly for the poor or weaker sections of the society in India or abroad.
  • To provide counseling and care services to the displaced poor women and children and their co-dependents.
  • To provide medical and care services to the poor aged people.
  • To provide counseling, referral and care services to the HIV/AIDS and STD infected and their co-dependents.
  • To facilitate, generate and design a program on women & child welfare upliftment issues.
  • To promote education in the rural areas.
  • To revive, preserve, protect and promote the cultural heritage and flora and fauna.

Programs and Service provided

COUNSELING CENTRE :


  • A vital part of the services provided by the Bethesda Youth Welfare Centre is the Chemical Addiction outpatient program which is designed to meet the needs of those who do not require in-patient assistance but who needs help in overcoming Substance abuse behaviour. Here the focus is on Chemical dependency counselling whether the problem involves Alcohol, Heroin and other addictive chemicals.
  • The counselling services are available in group, family, couples and individual sessions for all age group. The duration of the Counselling /treatment is determined on the individual basis between the counsellor and the client.
  • The Bethesda outpatient program is conducted through its Counselling Recreational Centre.

DE-ADDICTION & REHABILITATION :


  • 15 Bedded indoor de-toxification and pre-rehabilitation counseling program for one month.
  • Three to six months rehabilitation program based on four pronged approach viz.., physical Therapy, Spiritual Therapy, work and Social Therapy.

PEOPLE'S RESPONSE TO CLIMATE CHANGE AND CAPACITY BUILDING ON PATRIARCHAL STRUCTURE AND CRIMINAL JUSTICE SYSTEM REFORM IN NEI :


The Project’s, People’s Response to Climate Change & Capacity Building on Patriarchal Structure and Criminal Justice System Reform in NEI is a continuation project of the erstwhile project of Strengthening Collective Efforts in North East India (SCENEI) which was started in the year 2004 covering 18 villages in the initial project period but later on expanded to 20 villages at present in the Nagaland-Assam Border of Wokha district of Bhandari Sub Division, Nagaland. This project is being operated in partnership with NE NGO Dialogue Forum supported by Bread for the World (BfTW) Germany. The Major activities that are being carried out are as follows: -

  1. Promotion of Indigenous food & seeds:

    In the midst of the mysterious insects attacks that are occurring on the agricultural crops from time to time the promotion of the indigenous foods and seeds, which had withstood different climatic invasion and changes since time immemorial need to be encouraged to the rural farmers. Besides, the indigenous food and crops can grow well without the application of artificial fertilizers and insecticides-the agent of air pollution and the various negative implications.

  2. Formation of Food and Seed bank:

    In order to keep alive the continuity of the indigenous food and seeds formation of food and seed bank in every village is necessary. Different methods of preservation need to be identified in order to ensure safety of the crops.

  3. Promotion of SHGs & Economic Literacy:

    Promotion or formation Self Helps Groups especially rural women farmers not only helps them in their economic upliftment but also encourages them in organic agricultural practices. The SHGs are also facilitates in linkages with different financial institution to enable them to access to various financial assistance besides exploring them for marketing avenues for uninterrupted marketing activities. We also conduct exposure cum exchange knowledge program to the rural women farmers to different places in exchanging their different knowledge on cultivation experiences.

  4. Awareness Creation and Sensitization Program on Climate Change:

    Mass awareness campaign and sensitization program on climate change are being conducted at the community level from time to time. The rural communities are also being advocating on community forest conservation and management besides biodiversity conservation & rejuvenation activities during cultural programs and important events such as World Environment Day, Earth Day, Bio Diversity Conservation Day, etc.

TRAGETED INTERVENTION (IDUs) PROJECTS :


Bethesda Youth Welfare Centre has initiated Targeted Intervention Projects for Injecting Drug Users (IDUs) in 3 districts of Nagaland, which is functioning in Networking with Nagaland State AIDS Control Society and EHA/AVAHAN -Project ORCHID.

Objective:
  • To create a situation whereby the spread of HIV/AIDS among the targeted IDUs could be controlled.
  • To improve the quality of life by enhancing awareness level about HIV/AIDS and related issues among the IDUs/DUs/ General Population.
  • To deal with the attitude of IDUs with respect to use of safer method of injection/condom and bring the targeted to the Harm Reduction Program.

    • Activities under DICs :
    - Counseling and one to one interaction
    - Abscess Care/Treatment
    - STI Treatment
    - STD,HIV/AIDS referrals & Primary Medical Care and rehabilitation information
    - Advocacy Meeting & Focus Group Discussion (FGD)
    - Awareness Campaigns on Drug & HIV/AIDS
    - Condom Promotion and Needle & Syringe Exchange Program (NSEP)
    - IEC/Recreational materials procurement and distribution

RURAL HEALTH & EDUCATIONAL UPLIFTMENT PROGRAM :


  • Rural Health & Educational programme was started in 1999 eith thrust area on the interstate border of Assam Nagaland on the foothills of Wokha District.
  • The Center periodically conducts free medical camps and awareness on proper health care covering 15 village in the area and currently supporting a Rural Health Worker in Lio- Wokha area. A rural Elementary School is in progress and the academic session is functional since 2001.

SUBSTITUTION THERAPY PROJECT :


  • This project is an additional component of Harm Reduction program available in Nagaland. The project is funded by DFID focusing on reducing the rate of HIV infection amongst the IDUs (Injecting Drug Users).
  • This project aims at changing the behavioral pattern of drug use from injecting to oral. The targeted Injecting Drug Users will be motivated and convinced into substitution program which will gradually help them to give up injecting habits reducing the risk of HIV infection through unsafe use of injecting equipments. However this project was merged with the Normal TI Project since 2009-10 sessions onwards.

    • Core Activities:
    -Buprenorphine Dispensing
    -TB Treatment
    -Counseling
    -Family Meeting
    -Advocacy Programs
    -Focus Group Discussion
    -Referrals

PROMOTING PREVENTION, CARE AND MANAGEMENT ON DIABETES AMONGST INDIGENOUS PEOPLE AND EMPHASIZING ON DIABETIC FOOT CARE :

  • The Project was started in the year 2016 March in collaboration with World Diabetes Foundation, Gentofte, Denmark. It is mainly focuses on Promoting Prevention, Care and Management amongst indigenous people emphasizing on Diabetic Foot Care. Presently, it is working with 2 districts of Nagaland i.e Dimapur and Wokha covering 379 villages.
  • It also provides free space for a Children ministry called KIDS WORKSHIP CENTRE. With all the basic amenities available, the guests are made comfortable during their stay at the centre. The centre is also in the process of setting up a library hall cum internet café which will provide basic and necessary information’s.
  • This Project aims to reduce the mortality and morbidity a foot problems and foot amputation of indigenous people from diabetes and its complications.

    • Main Objectives are :
    • To prevent diabetes amongst indigenous people at the remote villages.
    • To enhance Care, Treatment and Management of Diabetes.
    • To established Diabetic Clinics with arrangement for Diabetic Foot Care at remote area.
    • To create capacity of Doctors and Paramedics on Diabetics Management and Foot Care through comprehensive training.

YRG CARE RESEARCH STUDY :

  • A research Study Project for “Integrated Care Clinic (ICC) for IDUs in India – a cluster randomized trial” supported by National Institute of Health (USA) implemented in collaboration with John Hopkins Medical Institution, USA endorsed by ICMR and NACO.
  • Bethesda in collaboration with YRG CARE, CHENNAI, Department of AIDS Control, Government of India and State Aids Control Societies (SACS) is implementing a cluster – randomized trial amongst IDU to test the following Hypothesis Making all the WHO-recommended services available at one centre will improve utilization of all services (cross-referrals) and thereby, reduce HIV Transmission.
  • The study is a multi-tiered randomized trial-until now, ethnography phase, baseline assessment of thousand IDU, randomization of sites into control and intervention arm of these study are over continuing from here all, prospective steps are to set up intergraded cares centres, measure process utilization for 24-30 months and then closed out with an impact assessment through another survey involving 1000 IDU (Biological and Behavioral) .
  • The Purpose of the research Study was to Know whether the service are enhanced and up taken to strengthen the existing programs.
